Discover Toronto, Canada

Top 3 Attractions

CN Tower

An unmatched skyline view from the LookOut Level, a romantic fine dining experience at the revolving 360 Restaurant and multiple museum exhibits throughout the tower.

Royal Ontario Museum

One of the world's great museums, in the heart of downtown Toronto. Home to more than 13 million objects spanning art, culture, and nature, ROM is one of the city's must-see attractions.

Art Gallery of Ontario

The AGO maintains a comprehensive collection of Contemporary art spanning from 1960s to the present, reflecting global developments in artistic practice across all media, including painting, sculpture, works on paper, photography, projection art and installation art.

Retail Therapy

Bloor-Yorkville

Bloor-Yorkville is Toronto's most iconic neighbourhood merging style, culture, art, and wellness, all within a few must-visit blocks.

Eaton Center

This unrivaled retail destination is located in the heart of Canada’s most populous city, featuring more than 200 shops.

Queen Street West

The area is a hub of the city’s best restaurants, cafés, independent boutiques, street art and galleries, and is a must-see for anyone visiting the city.

Local Markets

St. Lawrence Market

St. Lawrence Market has been the community's beating heart and culinary focal point of the city of Toronto. It's the living symbol and anchor of the neighbourhood and for the families who live here.

Kensington Market

Kensington Market is a multicultural marketplace known for its independent spirit, colourful shop fronts, vibrant murals, charismatic locals and people-friendly Pedestrian Sundays events.

Distillery Historic District

The Distillery Historic District is widely known as Canada’s premier arts, culture and entertainment destination. A place brimming with creativity and creative people, that can inspire dreams, and a place that can help them come true.
